About Me

I am an immersive media design student (on the computing track) at the University of Maryland. My art has always involved traditional pen and paper, and frequently paint, "trash" and cardboard, but my process has evolved and expanded over time, to include all kinds of virtual creation and modeling alongside my physical creations. I consider myself an artist and technologist, interested in both the virtual and physical. I now use 3D modeling, rigging, and animating to form subjects and environments for games and interactive works that I then program. I embrace both traditional art techniques like painting and sculpture, and 3D printing and robotics to create tangible works. Overall, I like to create visually appealing works that tell stories and challenge players, all in order to provide entertainment or emotional experiences in users.
